# Prospect series x website - the redesign pass (one-page plan)

Drafted 2026-07-18. Context: the knowaguy.com.au homepage (docs/iknowaguy-website/) turned out to be
the prospect series compressed - written independently, it converged on the same arc. The series was
drafted (2026-07-07/08) for a reader who has seen NOTHING; the website-as-catalyst means most
subscribers now arrive having seen everything once, quickly. This pass re-fits the series to that
reality. Rule held throughout: one source of truth, never fork.

## The job change (write this into the series' self-understanding)

- OLD job (PROSPECT-SERIES-PLAN.md): "change minds - persuasion arc."
- NEW job: the homepage changes the mind in one sitting; the drip DEEPENS and RIPENS - each piece
  is the full episode behind a beat the homepage trailered. Persuasion ORDER survives (it works for
  ripening too, and not every subscriber read the site deeply); assumed FAMILIARITY changes.

## Two subscriber populations - the phrasing guideline

(a) website opt-ins (saw the homepage) and (b) the original re-engagement/networking list (may never
have seen the site). So every echo-owning line must be OPTIONAL-CONTEXT - "if you've read the front
page, you've glimpsed this" - never "as you read on the site." No piece may REQUIRE the homepage.

## Structural decisions

1. **The opt-in moment (the real "wire the CTA" item).** The homepage's only CTA is "Let's talk" -
   there is NO path from homepage to drip. But the drip IS the ripening instrument for the
   not-ready-yet middle. Add a second-tier CTA in exactly two places: the soft off-ramp ("Seen
   enough? let's have a chat - or have it arrive a week at a time") and near the close. The library
   already has the "These arrive in your inbox too" callout - it needs an actual signup, not just
   the sentence. Keep "Let's talk" primary everywhere; the series is the runner-up ask, never a
   competing headline.
2. **Library = #8, one text.** The library mockup IS how-it-actually-works.md rendered (same 7
   steps) - but the copies have drifted (mockup step text is tighter in places, and the mockup's
   17-piece catalogue descriptions are new good copy with no .md home). Reconcile: the .md files
   stay canonical; fold the mockup's better lines back into how-it-actually-works.md; bank the
   catalogue deks (they can become each piece's `dek` or a catalogue field). ⚠ The mockup's
   catalogue OMITS #46 connection-isnt-charm - add it (18 pieces, not 17).
3. **Stale docs.** PROSPECT-SERIES-PLAN.md's run table still shows the pre-2026-07-09 order (#5 at
   position 1); the 07-09 reorder (feast leads, revisit → before #50) lives only in a comment
   inside revisit-your-big-picture.md. Update the plan table to the canonical run above. Library
   catalogue order ≠ send order is fine by design (phases group by theme).
4. **Scoring is now public.** The homepage openly carries scoring-on-attitude + the connection-rate
   figures (35% exceptional → 45-55% ours), which the series routing kept client-only (#33).
   DECISION: fine as-is - homepage carries the compressed public version; #33's depth stays in the
   client library. No change; just noting the boundary moved on purpose.
5. **Homepage-minted material with no series home** (deliberate absences, revisit post-launch):
   the rules/learning beat ("make that a rule from now on" - the parked "Change the rule, not the
   message" piece), and "better than being in the room" (transcript-trust claim, partially in
   #60). Homepage carries both for prospects; no launch-set additions.
