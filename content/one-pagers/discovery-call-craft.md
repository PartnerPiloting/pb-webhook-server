# What to say in the discovery call

Last piece dissolved the fear: you're not there to pitch, you're there to discover. Lovely - until you're actually on the call, thirty minutes stretching out ahead of you, and your mind goes blank. So here's the how.

Start with the one rule that carries the entire call: **questions are the answers.** You don't have to be interesting. You have to be interested. Ask good questions and let them do the talking - which, happily, is also the thing that stops you blurting out your life story in the first two minutes.

The call has two parts, and the order matters.

**First, connect.** Don't open with business - open like a human. A warm hello, a smile they can hear, something real: "How long have you been at it?" or a line about something you genuinely noticed on their profile. Ninety seconds of actual rapport. Not so long that you're stalling, not so short that it feels like you're ticking a box before the "real" conversation - this *is* the real conversation.

**Then, ask.** Now you're curious, properly curious, about their world. What they do and how they got into it. What's going well, what's a grind, who they tend to help. You'll find you barely have to talk about yourself at all - which is exactly right.

Of course, they'll eventually turn it back on you: "So what about you - what do you do?" You don't need a pitch for this. Keep it short and honest. And if you're early - if you don't really have a business yet - that's completely fine, and here's a version that works: *"Honestly, I'm a big believer in networking. I love finding out what other people do, and I'm looking for the gaps in the market. Right now I've got a full-time job, but I don't see that as forever - my background's in [X], so that's the direction I'm heading."* No apology, no over-explaining. An honest position is more than enough.

Now, here's how you tell whether this is someone worth staying close to. Listen for one thing. Do they turn the curiosity around - "right, that's me, now what about you? Who could I be introducing you to?" The people who become real nodes in a network do that almost without noticing. The ones who never once get curious about you? That's information too.

And then the move that makes the whole thing compound: **keep the first call short - half an hour - and make it entirely about them.** Resist the urge to cram your story in. Because precisely *because* you left yourself out, you've now got the most natural reason in the world to talk again: "Last time you mentioned [X] - I've been chewing on it since, and I think I know someone you should meet." The first call being all about them is exactly what earns the second. And the second is where it quietly becomes mutual.

Which points at the real secret of this whole business - the one nobody tells you. It was never the call that built your network. It's what you do in the gap *after* it. That's next.
