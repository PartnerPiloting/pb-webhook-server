# Become the person who knows a guy

Last piece left you on a promise: a single sentence you'll find yourself saying more and more. Here it is. Three words.

*"I know a guy."*

For all the grandeur of where we just were - the web of nodes, the referrals arriving down chains you can't see - the whole thing rests on something almost comically small. Someone, somewhere, mentions a problem. A gap they need to fill. A kind of person they've been trying to reach. And you are the one who says *I know a guy* - and then actually makes the introduction.

That's it. That's the entire engine, dressed up as a throwaway line.

And the most important part is *how* you do it: freely. No ledger, no "you'll owe me one," no quiet expectation of a favour returned. You make the introduction because you can, because it'll help two people you rate, and then you let go of it completely. Which - and this is the quiet magic of the whole thing - is precisely why it comes back to you.

Now here's the part I love most. Everything else in this series takes time. Choosing the right people, reaching out, earning the calls, building a node - that's months of patient work. This is not. **This you can start today.** This afternoon, before you've done a single other thing on the map. Because you almost certainly already know two people who ought to know each other. You just haven't made the introduction yet.

So make it. You don't need a network to start being the person who connects people - being that person is how the network begins.

And then it compounds, quietly, on its own. The more you build, the more people you know, the more often you're the one with the answer - until "I know a guy" stops being something you occasionally offer and becomes the thing people come to you *for*. You turn into the natural centre of a network. Not a name on the edge of someone else's - the person everyone thinks of first.

Which, when you strip away all ten steps, is what this was always about. Not a bigger contact list. Not a clever system. Becoming the sort of person who, when there's a need in the room, has the answer - and gives it away. The person everyone is glad to know.

So don't wait for the network. Start today. The next time someone describes a problem, don't just nod along - reach for the name, and make the introduction. Be the one who knows a guy.

There's one thing left - the small matter of doing all of this, for everyone, without it quietly taking over your life. That's the last piece.
