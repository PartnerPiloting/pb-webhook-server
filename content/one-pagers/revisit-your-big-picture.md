# Revisit your big picture

Before any of the how-to, start with the why. Yours.

You didn't go out on your own by accident. Somewhere behind it was a picture - a way you wanted your working life to look and feel. More freedom. Work that meant something. Enough income without your whole life vanishing into earning it. Hold that picture for a second, because everything that follows only matters in service of it.

Here's the trouble with a big picture, though: it's the first thing you lose.

Not because you stop caring - because you get busy. The work gets detailed. Clients, deadlines, the weeds of actually delivering. This very series will do it to you too: piece after piece of specifics, and somewhere in there you'll surface and think, "hang on - why am I doing all this again?" That question isn't a distraction. It's the most important one you'll ask. The answer lives up in the big picture, and you have to deliberately climb back up to see it.

And when you do, here's what you'll notice: the plan has changed. It always does.

You set out with a grand plan and, somewhere in the middle, a chunk of it goes to custard. A couple of things you were counting on just don't come off. That isn't failure - that's every plan ever made. And here's the quiet reassurance: the *destination* usually survives even when the route falls apart. If you started building your network early enough, you've got a bench of good people and warm leads ready to step in for whatever fell through. The plan flexed; you barely felt it. That's what a network really is - not a nice-to-have, but the shock absorber under every plan you'll ever make.

Which points at something worth seeing clearly. Your plans will keep changing shape. Maybe the picture is more income for steadily *less* of your time. Maybe it's retainer work - genuinely possible in the world we now live in. Maybe there's an app in you, or something you haven't thought of yet. Every one of those is a different *what*. But look underneath them and there's a single *how* that serves all of them: the network. It's the one thing that feeds every version of the dream.

So here is the one commitment that survives every change of plan: **a consistent amount of network building.** Whatever else shifts, that stays. It's where the leads come from, yes - but also the ideas, the encouragement, the person who shows you a door you didn't know was there. Keep that one thing steady and the picture always has somewhere to grow from.

And maybe let the big picture itself be simpler than a five-year plan. Most people, underneath all the specifics, want two things: to make a real difference to other people, and to have their time leveraged - to earn well without trading away every hour to do it. That's less a *what you'll do* than a *how you want to live*. The "what" can change a dozen times. The "how" is the thing to protect.

That's the picture worth coming back to, again and again - especially when the detail gets thick.

There's just one force that works against all of it. Quietly, structurally, every time you get busy. It's the reason most people's networks lapse at the exact moment they matter most. That's next.
