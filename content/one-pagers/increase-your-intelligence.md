# Increase your intelligence with Wingguy

There's a catch to this whole series, and you may have felt it building.

Everything works - the careful choosing, the personal note to every connection, the considered times instead of a link, the real curiosity on each call, a reason to follow up with everyone, an introduction whenever you can make one. Do all that for one or two people and it's a pleasure. Do it for the hundreds it takes to build a node network - properly, every week, for years - and it's a second full-time job. The kind of job that, the moment your real work gets busy, quietly doesn't get done. Which is exactly the see-saw we started with.

So either this whole beautiful method collapses back into feast-or-famine the first busy month... or something carries the load for you.

You've been watching that something the whole way through. Every time I said "the system reads their profile and drafts something genuinely insightful," or "it offers times that fit your calendar and books the call" - that was real, and it has a name. **Wingguy.**

It comes in two halves. There's the extension, which lives on LinkedIn: it reads each message, works out where the conversation has got to and what should happen next, drafts the reply in your voice, offers the times, books the meeting. And there's the assistant you simply talk to: "prep me for today's calls," and it briefs you from every past meeting; "draft the follow-up from that Zoom," and it writes it. It knows your calendar, your transcripts, your email - your whole working week, not just one channel.

And before you picture the usual bland AI sludge - it isn't that. What Wingguy writes is shaped by *your* rules, not some model's generic idea of a nice message. It arrives with a starting set - mine, built up over years - and from there it becomes yours: when a draft isn't quite right, you don't grit your teeth and rewrite it, you just tell it, in plain words, right there in the chat, what you'd have done differently - and it updates the rule. Every correction bends it a little closer to you. And it never had that off-the-shelf, AI-ish feel in the first place - the rules see to that; even on day one it reads like a person, not a machine. The tweaking just makes it yours: saying exactly what you'd want said, the way you'd want it said. You on a good day.

Here's the way to think about it, and it's the reason for the odd title. Wingguy isn't really a tool you operate. It's closer to being smarter and bigger than you are on your own - to *increasing your own intelligence*. You keep the part that has to be you: who's worth choosing, whether a draft rings true, who ought to meet whom. It carries everything that never needed you in the first place - the remembering, the drafting, the arranging, the quiet discipline of never dropping a single person. That is why a day that used to cost hours now costs minutes.

For what it's worth, I get back about three hours a day. Not by cutting corners - by handing the parts that were never really mine to do to something that does them in an instant, and keeping the parts that are.

And that is the piece that makes all the others possible. Everything in the last nine steps stops being "a lot" and starts being something you can genuinely sustain - across hundreds of people, busy month or quiet, without going cold and without burning out. Take it on, and every step on the map above starts, quietly, to run itself.

Which brings the whole thing full circle. We began with the see-saw - network building forever losing to the work that pays, because it was an *activity* that ate time you didn't have. It doesn't have to be that any more. Choose the right people. Be the one who knows a guy. And let Wingguy carry the rest - so your network keeps growing in your busiest week exactly as it does in your quietest. That was the promise on page one. This is how it's kept.
