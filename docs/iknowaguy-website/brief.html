The decision

Website-first

The website is the priority artifact - the thing you hand to referrals, paste into LinkedIn chats, and point cold prospects at. The prospect email series becomes a delivery channel of the same content, decided afterwards. They are not two projects; they are one body of writing with two ways of delivering it.

The one rule to protect: a single source of truth. The content pieces stay canonical; the website renders them and the emails render them. Improving a piece improves both at once. Never fork it into two divergent copies.

What the site is for

One asset, many jobs

Because the paradigm is universal, the same writing does acquisition and retention:

  • Referral hand-out  The thing a client sends to someone they rate. Powered by the $0-at-three-referrals incentive - motivated referrers who finally have something good to give.
  • "How does it work?" link  Dropped into LinkedIn connection / thanks-for-connecting chats when someone wants more.
  • Credibility check  For the cold prospect mid-outreach deciding whether to take the Zoom. (Replaces the harm ASH currently does here.)
  • Client reinforcement  Existing clients re-read the paradigm - conviction is what carries them through the flat part of the curve. This is a retention asset, not just acquisition.
  • Series home  The seed and permanent home for the prospect email series.

The scorecard

Earn the conversation - don't try to wow

The site can't reproduce your live demo, and shouldn't try - chasing "wow" tips into hype and fails the skeptic. The demo creates wow (capability, live). The site creates want (a vivid scene, the paradigm) - and want is what earns the conversation where you close.

North-star test for every decision: would a client be proud to send this - and would the smart friend they sent it to come away nodding?

Structure

Lean page, depth behind a link

The front page

  • Owns the why (the paradigm)
  • One vivid scene (the drafted-reply moment) - not a feature list
  • You, personally, front-and-centre - warm, modest, the founder behind a system
  • Opens on the hook (we avoid the thing we know works) + the honesty beat
  • Ends on a soft "let's talk" - never "sign up"
  • Build less - readable in under a minute

The library

  • The prospect series (outsider-safe paradigm pieces)
  • Led by the "how it actually works" map
  • Grouped by the argument, not a flat blog
  • Handles deeper objections (incl. "I could build that", AI-isn't-the-answer)
  • Craft / client pieces stay in the members' portal - not public

Voice & guardrails

Sound like you, disarm the skeptic

  • Guy across a coffee table - contractions, specific ($3,500), warm, peer-to-peer
  • Short dash ( - ), never em dash · "recommend", not "refer"
  • The honesty beats stay: "stick with what's working" and "no magic wand"
  • Affirm, then reframe - never "you're wasting your time" (especially to proud DIY-ers)
  • Every public surface passes the anti-MLM test
  • Sign-off: - (I know a) Guy

Look

The Connector's Study

Warm oat paper, espresso ink, a single deep petrol-teal accent (deliberately not hustle-gold or SaaS-blue), one restrained warm-ochre highlight under "I know a guy". Bookish serif for display, humanist sans for body. Light and dark. See the homepage and library mockups.

Sequence

What we do, in what order

  1. Reconcile the library home now One home for the writing, not two (WordPress vs the server). Resolved by building the one home - the website.
  2. Build the real front page + library now Off the existing content pieces as the source. Clean build, not WordPress.
  3. Fold new beats into the source now e.g. the "funnel is just the top" beat for #65 - flows to both website and emails.
  4. Wire the CTA soon A real way to talk to you (booking / contact).
  5. Email drip mechanics later Send order, per-audience endings, state - a delivery concern, deferred cleanly.
  6. Billing cutover + ASH redirect later PMPro → Stripe (mostly already built), then redirect the old domain.

Still open

Decisions parked for later

  • How "let's talk" actually works - booking link vs simple contact
  • The library one-line hooks need tuning to your intent
  • Whether "funnel is just the top" is a beat in #65 or its own piece
  • Confirm existing members are already live Stripe subscriptions (makes migration a mapping job)
