How it actually works

A map doesn't teach you the ground. It just shows you where everything is.

  1. 1

    Choose who's worth knowing

    Decide, on purpose, who belongs in your network before you meet anyone - not the people who might buy from you, but the ones positioned to know your work and recommend you. Chosen for how they think, not their job title.

    → Choose, Don't Collect
  2. 2

    Reach out like a human

    A genuine note about their work, not a pitch about yours. The whole posture is "let me find out what you do" - the opposite of the thing everyone braces for.

    → A thanks-for-connecting that delights
  3. 3

    Say thank you like you mean it

    When they connect back, there's a small window of goodwill. A genuine, specific line about their work - the kind that makes someone feel actually seen - turns a new contact into a real conversation.

    → A thanks-for-connecting that delights
  4. 4

    Have a conversation to learn

    The first call fills people with dread because they think it's a pitch they can fail. It isn't. You're there to learn about them - nothing to sell, so nothing to be rejected about. The easiest conversation in the world.

    → What can you discover?
  5. 5

    Give it a reason to continue

    Relationships live on motion, not goodwill. So you leave every one with a reason to come back - something to share, someone to introduce, a thread to pick up.

    → A reason to follow up
  6. 6

    Watch it grow into a web

    Do this with enough of the right people - the ones who build their own networks too - and you're not networking alone anymore. Work starts arriving on a recommendation, from people you've never even met.

    → Building nodes
  7. 7

    Become the one who knows a guy

    Underneath all of it, one small human habit: when someone mentions a need, you're the one who says "I know a guy" - and makes the introduction, freely, expecting nothing. Do that often enough, and you become the person everyone's glad to know.

    → Become the person who knows a guy
